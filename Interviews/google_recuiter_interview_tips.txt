Questions to Ask an Interviewer:
1. Always ask questions
	1. Not just at the end
	2. Throughout the interview (get insight into what they want for the role)
		1. Why? To show that we are interested in the role
			1. Big decision
		2. Apply the answers in future interviews as well
			1. To know the process
			2. To know what they are looking for
			3. Engaged in dialogue but run out of time is okay
			4. Not asking shows the candidate is not interested in the role
2. It is okay to go with a piece of paper with questions written down
	1. Shows a level of preparation
		1. Different lists can be prepared for different people
			1. 7 out of 20 ...
		2. Questions differ from hiring manager from team member, cross functional team member ...
3. Questions depend on interviewer and adapting to personality type
	1. Not all questions are appropriate
		1. Depends on role and personality type
			1. If someone likes to answer, can go a little deep
			2. If someone is cold and not answering questions much, do not dig deeper
		2. Adapting candidates can get hired
4. Examples:
	1. Get to know your interviewer personally
		1. What do they like about the company
		2. What do they like about their role
		3. What is that they think is great about the culture, environment
		4. What do they like about their position
		5. What is the career growth been like in the organization
		6. ... take interest in them
	2. Types of persons
		1. Big company - recruiter
		2. Big-medium sized company - HR, wrap recruiter
		3. Small company - hiring manager
	3. Questions:
		1. About the position
			1. Why is the position open?
			2. How long has the position been open?
			3. How long are the candidates been interviewing?
				1. If they can tell you
			4. Why do I think my background is a good fit?
			5. Logistics
				1. Location of the company
					1. Can I work out of another location?
					2. Can I work remotely?
					3. Does the job require travel?
			6. What is the salary range?
			7. What are the specific benefits including vacation, PTO, holidays, sick days, medical, dental and any other perks
			8. What is the process and how many steps do they have?
			9. How long is the process?
			10. How often will I hear back from you?
			11. Will you let me know if you are out on vacation?
				1. To know who the backup person is
			12. How many interviews are conducted?
			13. What are the stages?
			14. Who is the final decision maker?
			15. What topics will be covered in the interviews? (upfront)
		2. In the interview:
			1. Know about them
	4. Hiring manager:
		1. I looked you up on LinkedIn but tell me about your career path (learning about them)
		2. How can hiring for this position help with your biggest challenges, success look like (6 months, 2 years), typical career path for this position, fit in the overall strategy of the organization, typical day in this role, clarify/expand on you - are there any questions from the way I have answered today or from my background that I have answered for you (critical question)
			1. Make sure nothing is left lingering
		3. Any additional skills they see to be a fit for the role
